Key Developmental Milestones - Pregnancy

Prenatal Development

  • First Trimester (0-3 months)
    • Zygote (1-2 weeks) – implantation
    • Embryo (3-8 weeks) – essential organs develop; embryo very susceptible to teratogens
    • Fetus (9-12 weeks) – grows a lot; behaviors appear
  • Second Trimester (3-6 months)
    • Grows; movements detected; eyes sensitive to light; reacts to sound
  • Third Trimester (6-9 months)
    • Size increases; fetus gets in upside-down position for birth

Childbirth - three stages of labor

  • Effacement & Dilation of Cervix and Transition
    • Contractions - muscles of uterus contract
    • Cervical effacement – thinning, shortening, and drawing up of the cervix (0-100%)
    • Cervical dilation – thinning and opening of the cervix (0-10 cm)
  • Pushing and Birth of Baby
  • Delivery of the Placenta

Concerns Relevant for Transition to Parenthood and Pregnancy

The transition to parenthood can be difficult as it leads to changes in love and leisure time with your spouse and changes in employment for one or both parents.

  • Research suggests that finding time to spend together as a couple, teamwork and accepting new roles have a positive impact on transitioning to parenting. I plan to find time to spend alone with my husband once a week so that we can get that time together and be able to enjoy more fully the other responsibilities that we’ll have as parents. I plan to discuss with him our new roles.

Infertility can be a struggle for a couple who wishes to become parents. About 1/10 couples struggle with it.

  • I hope to be sensitive around others who may be facing this struggle. If my husband and I struggle with infertility, I plan to go to an infertility doctor to see what we can do to treat it. If we struggle for a long time with it, I plan to adopt children. Research shows that it can be treat with medication for women to induce ovulation, intrauterine inseminations or assisted reproductive technology.

Pregnant mothers must be particularly careful about what they ingest while pregnant and breastfeeding.

  • I plan to follow closely the guidelines about what to eat and what not to eat while pregnant and breastfeeding. I will avoid drugs that aren’t prescribed by a doctor, raw food, hot dogs/lunch meat, unpasteurized dairy, fish and supplements.

"The following guidelines for pregnancy weight gain are based on a woman's BMI (body mass index) before becoming pregnant with one baby:

  • Underweight: Gain 28-40 pounds
  • Normal weight: Gain 25-35 pounds
  • Overweight: Gain 15-25 pounds
  • Obese: Gain 11-20 pounds"

Walker, 2017
